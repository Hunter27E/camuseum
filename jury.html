Jury

Edith Blanchard

Director of the Charles H. MacNider Museum

Edith Blanchard is currently the Director of the Charles H. MacNider Museum in Mason City, Iowa. She received her master‘s degree in Museum Studies from Texas Tech University and has been involved in the Museum field for over 20 years. During her time at the MacNider the Museum has expanded its collection of artwork that focuses on persons of color as well as women. As a child Edith attended arts classes at the MacNider. Due to enrollment in a small school with little arts funding, it was here that she found her calling. Her aunt and uncle both worked at Living History Farms, a large living history museum, where she would visit in the summer. Having a learning disability Edith soon discovered that through hands-on non-traditional methods of teaching, she understood what she could previously could not. She determined at an early age that working in a museum, teaching others through non-traditional methods, was where she found her passion. Through her undergraduate degree she mostly worked in clay, taking 3 years of ceramics at Iowa State University while an undergraduate. Prior to coming to the MacNider in 2007 she worked as the Curator for the National Czech and Slovak Museum and Library where she developed a particular interest in the artist Andy Warhol, whose family were immigrants from what was then Czechoslovakia. Edith practices art herself selling handmade jewelry at summer art markets and is also fiber artist spinning wool and creating handmade quilts and textiles.

Ida Ivanka Kubler

Artistic Director

Ida Ivanka Kubler was born in South Bulgaria close to the border to Greece in 1978. Growing up in the sericulture for silk cocoons of her grandparents gave her the inspiration to use cocoons and other natural materials in her artworks. She started going to art lessons when she was 5 years old. In her adult life she had a traditional figurative art training at the National Academy of Arts in Sofia, Bulgaria. Ida states: "We would draw and learn about the human anatomy for 9 hours per day, every day of the week." She holds a BA in Product Design and Marketing from the University of Applied Sciences in Bielefeld, Germany. Her most prominent series she started during her PgDip studies in Fine Art from Chelsea College of Arts, London, UK. These are abstract series made from recycled silk cocoons naturally left by the butterflies. Ida has had art studios internationally in seven countries: Bulgaria, Germany, England, France, Norway, Italy and USA. Her artworks have found homes in Australia, India, Spain, Switzerland, Germany, Guernsey, Norway, England and USA. She states: "The main motive for my travels was love: either for the arts or for individuals. I spent time with the families of my art collectors, I organized shows. It was the search for beauty and the discovery of beauty in environments and relationships, which is my main driving force. I believe I still kept my Slavic soul." On a personal trip to Iowa, Ida met Lori Willert, the director of the Bolan's nonprofit, and together they started growing the seed of this idea of advancing the culture in Bolan, Iowa but also empowering and supporting the artists by giving them the opportunity to show their art in a museum. "I felt that I could act as a bridge between the warm, sweet people from Iowa and artists from around the World. It is a win-win cultural exchange, which will be empowering for both sides."

Maia Dimitrova

Art Collector and Art Dealer

Maia Dimitrova is originally from Bulgaria. Maia studied and lived in Poland, Czech Republic, Hungary and the United States. She is holds MA in Slavic Studies from Sofia University "St. Kliment Ohridski", in Sofia, Bulgaria, where she graduated with the highest honor. After moving to the United States, Maia completed her second master's degree in International Affairs at New York University in New York, USA. Maia's deep art, historical and cultural knowledge of Russia, Eastern and Western Europe and the United States and her passion for art, prompted her to immerse into the art world first as an art collector, and later as an art dealer and art gallery owner. Working as an art dealer and gallery owner enabled her to work with artists with different styles, from different countries and at different stages of their career development. In addition to her devotion to fine arts, Maia's interests include interior design and interior decoration in which artwork presence, in her view is indispensable. In addition to English, Maia speaks Russian, Czech, Bulgarian and French. Paul Huntington.

Paul Huntington

Art Collector and Art Dealer

Paul Huntington has worked at the arts funding and grant giving organization: the Greater London Arts Association. This organization selected applying arts groups and individuals for funding and distributed arts grants in the Greater London area. Paul has a significant interest in the visual arts. He set up the Workbox Gallery in Chelsea, London, a gallery selected by the prominent Volta Art Fair in New York. He has been involved in a number of galleries and has been influential in the careers of artists acting as an art advisor and art promoter. He has also worked at the Donmar Warehouse in Covent Garden. Paul has additionally worked and studied at City University and the University College London (UCL) where he was employed as a Senior Research Fellow in the department of Information Science. Paul’s interests are wide and he has attended many gallery openings and art lectures.

Pedda Borowski

Artist

Pedda Borowski is known as a visual artist, draftsman and painter, also in collaboration with other art disciplines such as music and dance as a performance artist. Borowski also teaches as a freelance lecturer in the teaching areas of artistic anatomy and spatial representations. He is also active as a Shiatsu practitioner GSD. Borowski received a diploma in drawing from the University for Applied Sciences in Bielefeld. After several years abroad (Paris/France and Cairo/Egypt) he lives and works in Berlin/Germany. In his current art he dedicates himself to the human figure. A series of large-format paintings is being created, the content of which is devoted, among other things, to the meridians known from traditional Chinese medicine. Another major artistic field of interest are paintings with erotic depictions of the body, which are in the tradition of his "artistic and spiritual parents". Georgia O'Keeffe, Anais Nin, Egon Schiele, Lucian Freud, August Rodin. Before turning to the human figure, Borowski painted spaces and architecture. The template for this long-lasting artistic period was the literature of Franz Kafka. Borowski understood these paintings as film stills of imaginary, i.e. never-shot films, in which Kafka's literature functioned as scripts. Working title of the painting "Silent film with voice." Borowski in the O-Ton: "I understand myself with my art not only as 'solo self-employed' but am very interested in connections with the protagonists of the Berlin art and cultural scene coming from all over the world. I especially like to make my nets with dancers and musicians!"
