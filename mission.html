Our Mission

LORI 'NYDEGGER' WILLERT

Director of the Bolan Museum

In my role I aim to connect the past of Bolan with the new spirit of the present and future, enrich culture and create a platform for communication.

Historically, the village of Bolan originated more than a century ago when the Chicago Great Western Railroad track was laid. The town was given the name Bolan in honor of the railroad master Matt Bolan in 1886. While the rail is no longer in use and the town is not a sprawling metropolis, Bolan has a large population of loyal and sincere supporters.

Whether people grew up here, lived on a neighboring rural acreage, attended Barton No. 2 School, patronized the businesses, worshiped at Trinity United Methodist Church, lived in a house for a couple years or life time, joined the community during one of its many celebrations, participated in Vacation Bible School, or briefly visited its park, this place feels like home. No matter how brief the stay or visit, it is a memorable community. Bolan is a place where friends meet, perhaps for the first time or several times over the years.

It is inspirational to read the "Bolan News" of years ago, and to hear stories passed down from the previous generations. This has been a place of fellowship and education; persistence and progress; faith, hope and love for many years. It brings me great satisfaction to contribute toward continuing that legacy by volunteering as chairperson of our nonprofit organization.

Working with residents and local citizens on these projects have improved our location and enhanced our quality of life. I love that about Bolan and living here with my family! Community members work to improve their own space, as individuals and families, while being provided their privacy. At the same time, opportunities for people to work together inspire more ideas and build lasting friendships that benefit our town and nearby communities as well.

The landscape is beautifully lush and green with fields and gardens in the summer, while amazingly serene and gorgeous, covered with a blanket of white snow in the winter. In this atmosphere of tranquility and warm welcoming, in the middle of the corn fields of Iowa, we are planting these seeds and hoping they will grow to an opulent harvest.

As an avid Bolan community promoter, I am excited to introduce to you the BOLAN CONTEMPORARY ART projects at the BOLAN MUSEUM housed in the BOLAN SCHOOLHOUSE & CULTURAL CENTER. This undertaking is the start of an exciting journey between art, cultural exchange, communication and new notions for something even bigger. We are going on this journey with the hope to assist local, national and international artists to "meet" through Bolan and give them a chance to advance in their career and personal development. At the same time, give the people in the Bolan area the opportunity to welcome new contemporary cultural impulses. The cultural exchange will be exciting and very rewarding.

The eleven board members were quick to offer endorsement and support to move forward with the plans for the BOLAN CONTEMPORARY ART projects because this aligns well with the mission, and purpose, "To raise awareness of local, national and international artwork and artists."

The Bolan Schoolhouse & Cultural Center mission is, "To restore, preserve, and maintain the 1923 Barton No. 2 Schoolhouse as a center that shares educational history, hosts cultural experiences, and continues to promote Bolan as "a place where friends meet."
